Langston's Lineage

Saba

Saba, born Tahj Malik Chandler in 1994 in Chicago, Illinois, is a highly acclaimed rapper, singer, and producer known for his introspective lyricism, and soulful production. Saba's discography reflects his exploration of African American identity and the societal issues that impact his community. Through his music, he delves into the nuances of his own experiences and the broader struggles faced by African Americans, offering a candid portrayal of life in urban America. From his early mixtapes like "GETCOMFORTable" to his acclaimed albums like "Bucket List Project" and "Care for Me," Saba has used his platform to shed light on the realities of growing up in marginalized communities, while also celebrating the resilience and creativity of the African American spirit.

Saba's Discography

"Bucket List Project" is a coming-of-age album that reflects Saba's
experiences growing up as a young African American man in Chicago. Tracks like
"Church / Liquor Store," "Stoney," and "Westside Bound 3" offer poignant reflections
on the challenges and triumphs of navigating life in marginalized communities. Saba's
honest portrayal of his personal struggles and aspirations resonates with listeners,
providing a voice for African American youth grappling with similar issues of identity,
community, and belonging.

"PROM / KING" is a standout track from Saba's album "Bucket List Project"
that explores themes of loss, grief, and the complexities of family relationships.
The song, which reflects on the tragic death of Saba's cousin and the impact it had
on his family, offers a raw and emotional portrayal of the realities of growing
up in urban America. Saba's introspective lyricism and heartfelt delivery resonate
deeply with African American listeners, providing a poignant reflection of the pain and resilience within the black community.

"LIFE" is a standout track from Saba's album "Care for Me" that addresses
themes of survival, resilience, and the pursuit of happiness in the face of
adversity. The song reflects on the impact of violence and systemic injustice
on African American communities, offering a poignant reflection of the realities
of life in urban America. Saba's introspective lyricism and emotive delivery
provide a voice for African American youth navigating the complexities of
their identity and experiences, fostering a sense of empowerment and solidarity
within the community.
