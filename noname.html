Langston's Lineage

Noname

Noname, born Fatimah Warner in 1991 in Chicago, Illinois, is a highly respected rapper, poet, and activist known for her introspective lyricism, intricate wordplay, and unique blend of jazz-infused rap. Emerging from the vibrant Chicago music scene, Noname's music is characterized by its poetic depth, socially conscious themes, and soulful production, making her a standout artist in contemporary hip-hop. One rapper profoundly influenced by Noname's groundbreaking work is Saba, a fellow Chicago native and rising star in the hip-hop scene. Saba, born Tahj Malik Chandler, has openly cited Noname as a major influence on his artistic development and musical style.

Noname's Discography

"Telefone" is a landmark project that showcases Noname's poetic
lyricism and soulful sound. The mixtape explores themes of self-discovery,
love, and the challenges of navigating life in Chicago. Saba has praised the
project for its honesty and vulnerability, qualities that resonate with his own
approach to storytelling. Saba's debut studio album, "Bucket List Project,"
released in the same year as "Telefone," shares a similar introspective quality,
tackling personal experiences and aspirations.

Noname collaborated with Saba on the track "Shadow Man"
from her mixtape "Telefone." The synergy between Noname and Saba
on this track showcases a seamless blend of their lyrical abilities and
shared commitment to addressing societal issues. The collaborative spirit
evident in "Shadow Man" reflects Noname's influence on Saba's own collaborative
projects, fostering a sense of community within the Chicago hip-hop scene.

Open Apology is a song that Noname collaborated with Saba on.
